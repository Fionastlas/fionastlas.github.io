What This Is

This is not coaching. It is not therapy. It is something more precise.

Self Discovery Atlas is a private advisory practice for high-performing leaders navigating the gap between external success and internal alignment.

The kind of gap that doesn't show up in your metrics. The kind that shows up at 2am, or in the moment after a win that should feel bigger than it does.

Fiona works with founders, executives, and creative entrepreneurs who are done performing their ambition — and ready to build from a different place entirely.

This is structural inner work. Methodical. Deep. Permanently integrating.

Who It's For

Built for a specific kind of person.

You are highly capable — and that capability has become its own kind of trap.

You move fast, decide well, and deliver consistently. But internally, something has quietly shifted. Rest feels like falling behind. Clarity feels harder to access than it used to. The version of success you're building no longer maps perfectly to the version of yourself you're becoming.

You don't need to be fixed. You need a framework for what's actually happening — and a practice for moving through it with intention.

Self Discovery Atlas is for leaders who are ready to do that work.

Origin

A practice built from the inside out.

Psychotherapist. Artist.
Founder of Self Discovery Atlas.

  • Clinical psychotherapy training
  • Founder, Self Discovery Atlas
  • Featured in Voyage Miami
  • Creator, The Moon Series (ongoing)

I trained as a psychotherapist. I spent years working at the level most people never go — sitting with high-functioning humans in the space beneath the persona, beneath the performance, beneath the story they've been telling themselves about who they are.

What I kept seeing, over and over, was this:

The most capable people in the room were often the loneliest in their own inner lives.

Not because they lacked intelligence or self-awareness. They had plenty of both. But because the very systems that made them successful — the discipline, the drive, the ability to suppress and override — were also the systems standing between them and the version of themselves they were quietly trying to become.

I built Self Discovery Atlas for them. For you.

This isn't therapy. And it isn't coaching.

Therapy looks backward to heal. Coaching looks forward to perform. What I do lives in a different register entirely.

I work with the underlying structure — the belief architecture, the identity constructs, the unconscious agreements you've made about what you're allowed to want, who you're allowed to be, and how much is enough.

When that structure changes, everything built on top of it changes. Not gradually. Structurally.

That's the work.

The foundation

I've spent years at the intersection of psychology, creative practice, and high-performance environments — developing a methodology that honors both the precision of clinical training and the fluidity of lived experience.

My work has been featured in Voyage Miami.

The Moon Series — an ongoing body of artwork — is a parallel expression of this practice: an exploration of cycles, visibility, and what it means to be fully seen.

If any of this resonates — not just intellectually, but in that quieter place — I'd welcome a conversation.

Who This Is For

This engagement is built for a specific threshold.

You are at the top of your field — or approaching it. You have built real things: companies, careers, creative bodies of work, reputations that precede you.

And something has shifted.

The drive is still there, but it's running on a different kind of fuel than it used to. Achievement still happens, but the meaning is harder to locate. You find yourself asking questions that don't have obvious answers — about legacy, about alignment, about who you actually are beneath what you've built.

This is not a crisis. This is an invitation.

Self Discovery Atlas exists for leaders who are ready to answer it. You may be:

  • — A founder or post-exit entrepreneur in a season of reinvention
  • — A senior executive navigating a significant internal or professional shift
  • — A high-performing creative whose outer output has outpaced inner coherence
  • — A public-facing leader who needs a private space for genuine depth
The Methodology

The Conscious Self-Actualization Method

An 8-pillar framework developed over years of clinical work, research, and direct engagement with high-performing individuals.

01
Radical self-honesty without shame

Seeing clearly without collapsing. The foundation of everything.

02
Trigger intelligence & blind spot illumination

Understanding your reactive patterns at the structural level — not just managing them.

03
Mastery of inner game to reduce external friction

How internal clarity translates directly into sharper decisions, fewer conflicts, better leadership.

04
Vitality reintegration

Reclaiming the energy that performance has quietly been borrowing from.

05
Ambition alignment

Separating who you are from what you've achieved — and rebuilding ambition on that distinction.

06
Peace as strategic anchor

Not stillness as retreat. Peace as the most powerful operating state available to you.

07
Existential integration

Meaning. Legacy. Multi-generational impact. The questions that don't go away — addressed directly.

08
Conscious decision architecture

How you make choices when operating from integrated clarity rather than conditioned reaction.

The Structure

The structure of this work

This is a minimum 3-month engagement, designed for depth — not quick fixes.

The first six weeks are intensive: weekly 60-minute sessions focused on deep mapping.

Beyond that, we build the cadence that serves your evolution — not a one-size framework.

Maximum five concurrent clients. This is by design.

Every engagement includes
  • Weekly 60-minute sessions (first 6 weeks)
  • Voice note access Monday–Friday for integration support between sessions
  • Quarterly deep-dive vision recalibration
  • Personalized integration rituals developed specifically for you

No public pricing. This practice does not operate on open enrollment. Engagements begin on a rolling basis as space becomes available.
